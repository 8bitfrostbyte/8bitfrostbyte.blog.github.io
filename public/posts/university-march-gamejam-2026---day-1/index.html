GameJam Day One: It Begins again

3 March 2026·1478 words·7 mins Draft
Author
Print(“Hello World”)

Welcome to the March 2026 university Gamejam blog!
#

This post marks the start of the second university gamejam i have taken part in with the previous being in November and documented in other blog posts.

The jam started on a Monday and Just like last time, i arrived bright but this time not so early getting to the lab after the theme had already been announced and teams decided upon which put me at a severe disadvantage. This was because i had misheard the start time and the jam which i thought would start at 12pm (like last time) actually started at 10am. luckily, a classmate of mine who was also attending the gamejam and had been a previous team member in Novemebers competition had turned up at the correct time and assigned me to his team saving me the huge problem of finding an available spot.

As i arrived i was greeted with an old friend and former teammate Joel from the first University (and my first ever) gamejam (which we won) who had been asked to come in to give a talk and lend a hand since he had experience as not only a university alumni having been on the same course as me but also the creator of his own indie game called BladeRush a fast paced action game which he had released on Steam some months prior.

Next, i discovered the theme and realised that unlike in November where the theme was very static, this time the theme was something a lot more creative…

SPACE!
#

Now, of course my brain went straight to the obvious, stars, planets, exploration and the deep cold void of instant death that seperates us from alien life, i wondered what i could come up with and that’s when i turned to my three other team members and asked them two questions:

  1. How long do we have to make the game itself?

  2. What were we going to make?

After being told we only had three days in total with the jam ending on the wednesday at 12pm we started to brainstorm. At first there were no ideas at all. But then, someone chimed in with the idea for a creative take on the word itself, an acronym…

S.P.A.C.E

When we questioned what it standed for he replied with the most outrageous sentence not even i could have thought of in relation to the theme. He said…

Spaghetti
#

Pasta
#

and
#

Cheese
#

Evolution
#

I will admit when first hearing what it meant i was very confused and asked whether this was actually going to be our idea or whether he was joking. Fully ready to argue against the whole idea, it was explained to me that the idea focused around playing as an evolutionised tiny piece of cheese that had grown legs, arms and somehow gained self awareness. With this information the other memebers of the team agreed and without knowing how this would turn out our idea was born and work began…

The first and most important task to any project especially with such a tight deadline is to assign the tasks for each memeber. In this case, rather than assigning a large number of smaller tasks we decided to allocate roles so there would be a specific role for each member and that would outline the tasks necessary for the three days we had.

However, before we could even begin, we encountered our first major issue. There was no version control meaning we could not begin work and when asked the head of the competition told us it should be ready soon. At the time, we didnt realise how long “soon” was going to be…

So for the time being i was assigned to gather assets for the project while the role of programmer, core programmer and level designer were assigned to the other three team memebers Damon, Josh and Liam.

While we all began our seperate first tasks, we began to flesh out our idea a bit, it was decided (eventually) that the player should be trapped in an oversized (to a piece of tiny self aware cheese) house which would fit with the “Space” theme (technically) since it wasn’t stated that it explicitly meant outer space. We checked with the head of the jam and got approval, with our idea having some substence we continued with our tasks.

After an hour we checked yet again if version control had been set up and yet again were told “soon”. However, during that time the game had begun to take it’s first steps into existence with Damon programming a rope swing mechanic designed around spaghetti that he called the “spag-rope swing”.

At this point (11am) i had yet to find any assets that were cohesive and Josh and Liam were still working on their respective sections of development such as the creation of the character we called Richard Cheese III (Bonus points if you get the joke) and further 3D model asset gathering. It was only later on in development that my more important second main task would be able to be started.

But until then, back to asset gathering i went.

for the next couple of hours i resigned myself to finding assets that fit the one room we thought would be the most fitting…

The Kitchen!

I thought this would be easy but after those couple of hours the assets i had managed to find were only semi cohesive and we lacked a definitive art style that also fit our unique character design which by now had been completed by Josh.

Regardless however, i gathered the bare minimum, a fridge, oven, table and chairs (cabinets came later) and then took a break because at this point it was 1pm and food had arrive.

Around 2pm, we decided to get back to work and as you can already guess, version control was still inactive. Met with “they are working on it” (another way of saying “soon”) we decided to focus more on the outline of game goals and came up with these questions:

  1. What are the hazards of the environment?

  2. What was the end goal of the game?

  3. How big should the enviroment be?

  4. What enviromental elements should be implemented?

To answer the first question, we asked ourselves what kind of threats would cheese face if it was a living being? Eventually, we settled on a single giant rat which i found from sketchfab a website for 3D models. The idea was to have the rat chase the player if they were on the ground and eventually kill the player if it go to close an idea that morphed slightly throughout development.

Simultaneously, we agreed the end goal should to be to collect pieces of cheese around the kitchen with completion being achieved if all cheese pieces had been collected without dying to the giant rat with death meaning complete restart of the level.

Within the level itself, it was decided that jump pads that would launch the player into the air made to look like pieces of lasgna and the spag-rope swing that would be used to clear a large gap between the counter and fridge would be our main enviromental elements and as a result i suggested that the kitchen should be extremely big to compensate the jump height and other elements involved.

Damon then suggested a slide made of pasta specifically macaroni a tube shaped pasta which the player would use to go to the ground and something i would create in 3Ds Max by using a spring object and inverting the normals so that there would be a useable interior.

All of this took up a lot of time and 6 hours after we had started at 4pm we were finally told version control had been set up so we quickly uploaded our work to perforce and setup our workspaces which would allow us to make changes and work simultaneously on the project as a whole ready for day two.

With all that completed, we decided to call it a day and head home where some members would work on setting up workspaces at home pcs for night work.

We all agreed to meet early the next morning at the lab to continue bringing the unique idea of S.P.A.C.E to completion. However, Liam decided to work from home for the remaining days communicating via Discord as it would take him over an hour to reach the lab and we all agreed it would be easier for him.

And so Day 1 came to an end with the beginings of what would later become the most unique project i have ever worked on (to date) and along came day 2, a day that would shape the final design of the game and see me starting my 2nd major role.
